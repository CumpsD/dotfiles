            Jonathan Cross ankostis • a year ago

            In the final step, if you email the key back to them, it is *their responsibility* to decrypt, then upload their key to a public server. If there is any kind of mistake in the email address, or they can't decrypt the message, then you can be sure the signature is of no use to anyone (as it should be).

            It is a way of *being sure* that the person still controls that email address and the private key corresponding to the public key you just signed.

            If you just upload their key to a keyserver, you skip that important check.
